NBA Bubble GIS Project

College Project
Summer 2020

The NBA Bubble Graphical Informational Systems (GIS) Project shows logistical information about the Morgantown, West Virginia area in regards to the NBA restarting their NBA season in this bubble location. I completed this project during a graduate course I took on GIS. This class and projeect allowed me to learn about several GIS operations and how to use them to solve spatial problems.

Overview

  • Description: The NBA Bubble Graphical Informational Systems (GIS) Project shows logistical information about the Morgantown, West Virginia area in regards to the NBA restarting their NBA season in this bubble location.
  • Results: I was able to successfully complete the project and answer the spatial question of what the logistics of restarting the NBA season in Morgantown, West Virginia would look like.
  • Impact: While this project was not used by the NBA directly, similar projects could be created to have a significant positive impact on NBA planning.


    • Features

    • Display logistics for this bubble location for the NBA to safely operate in
    • Clicking on various locations to learn more about them
    • Defining safe buffer areas for the players to freely travel within close to their hotels
    • Plan out available hotels, gyms, basketball courts, and travel paths within the bubble
